Box Plots (as rendered by R) contain the following features:

The Quartile Boxes
The plot consists of two adjoining boxes that extend out to the 25% and 75% confidence interval regions, respectively. 50% of all values in the sample population fall within the range of these two boxes, also known as the Interquartile Range (IQR)
Median Line
The Median Line lies where the two boxes join, indicating the 50% confidence mark where the most points in the sample population land. This line is many times not in the center, being skewed left or skewed right within the two quartiles.
Range 'Whiskers'
Two lines run out from the quartile boxes, extending to the smallest non-outlier value and the largest non-outlier value. The end points of the whiskers are determined by a range rule, which by default is a value 1.5 times the IQR value (Q3 - Q1) from the outer edge of the quartile box.
Outlier Dots (Optional)
If outliers are selected for the plot, then all other points that do not apply to the established 'range' of values are displayed as a dot or small circle to the extremes. These values are atypical representations of the sample population as a whole, but can offer insights into anomalies.
